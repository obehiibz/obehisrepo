Located off the coast if Queensland, Australia, the Great Barrier Reef is the largest coral reef system in the world. It stretches over 2,300 Kilometers (1,400 miles) and is home to a diverse array of marine life, including more than 1,500 species of fish, 411 types of hard coral, and 30 species of Whales and dolphins.
